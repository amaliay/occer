What is the problem?

Water is very important part of the life all living things in the world. Animals, plants, and humans need water to survive. The usage of clean water from year to year in each country is increasing, according to increasing population growth. Icreased use of clean water can be illustrated by the picture beside.

Fresh water gets special attention from the world, it can be proved by an international day to celebrate fresh water by The United Nation on March 22nd as World Day for Water. In addition, the world's attention to fresh water is also reflected in the sixth SDG's (Sustainable Development Goals) for Clean Water and Sanitation.



The rapid growth of population causes the need for clean water increased and also increase the waste produced by human activities pollute the water. Therefore, it can reducing the supply of fresh water. The United Nations estimates that the amount of waste water produced annually is about 1,500 km3, six times more water than exists in all the river of the world (UN WWAP,2003). Decline in the supply of clean water sources can be illustrated by the diagram below.

One of the human activities related to the use of water and produce water and produce waste that pollutes water is washing, where the waste produced is detergent waste. Bagai (2018) explain that water pollution by detergents is very dangerous because it contain approximately 35% to 75% phosphate salts that can cause a variety of water pollution problems. In addition, many detergent users use exceess detergent and more than the recommended amount.

Therefore, it is necessary to set precisely how much detergent is needed and waste treatment efforts to reduce the amount of detergent waste.
